About

I'm a PhD researcher in the Physics and Astronomy department at the University of Leicester, supervised by Prof. Nigel Bannister. My thesis, Novel Methods of Short-Wavelength Infrared Optical Navigation, develops a complete autonomous navigation pipeline for spacecraft operating in the SWIR waveband (0.9–1.7 μm) using InGaAs focal-plane arrays.

The central contribution is stellar–limb optical navigation (SL-OpNav) — a method that fuses star field observations with detected celestial body limbs to autonomously determine spacecraft position, refined through a triaxial ellipsoid body model and extended Kalman filter (EKF) state estimation. Around this sits a purpose-built pipeline: synthetic SWIR scene generation, a plate solver optimised for sparse near-IR star fields against the 2MASS catalogue, and instrument design trade-off analysis. The entire pipeline is designed for onboard execution, with algorithms validated on embedded hardware to demonstrate real-time autonomous operation under the computational constraints of spaceflight.
